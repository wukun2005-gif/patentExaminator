【Title】
Thermal Management Device for Semiconductor Light Sources

【Technical Field】
[0001] The present invention relates to thermal management of semiconductor light sources, and more particularly to a thermal management device utilizing phase change materials.

【Background】
[0002] High-power semiconductor light sources generate significant heat during operation. Existing thermal management solutions include passive heat sinks with fins, active cooling with fans, and liquid cooling systems.

[0003] US Patent Application US2020/0012345A1 describes a heat sink with aluminum fins attached to a base plate via brazing. While effective for moderate power levels, the thermal interface resistance between the base and fins limits performance at higher power densities.

【Summary of Invention】
[0004] The present invention provides a thermal management device comprising:
   a sealed housing defining an internal chamber;
   a phase change material disposed within the internal chamber;
   a thermally conductive substrate mounted on an upper surface of the housing;
   a semiconductor light source mounted on the substrate;
   wherein the phase change material has a melting point between 40°C and 70°C.

[0005] The sealed housing may be formed of copper or aluminum alloy. The internal chamber may contain a porous metal foam structure to enhance thermal conduction within the phase change material.

[0006] The phase change material may be a paraffin-based material or a salt hydrate material with a latent heat of fusion of at least 180 kJ/kg.

[0007] The thermally conductive substrate may be an aluminum nitride ceramic substrate or a direct copper bond (DCB) substrate with a thickness of 0.5-3mm.

【Detailed Description】
[0008] In one embodiment, the sealed housing is formed of copper with dimensions 100mm × 100mm × 20mm. The internal chamber contains paraffin wax with a melting point of 55°C and latent heat of fusion 220 kJ/kg. A copper foam structure (porosity 92%, pore size 0.8mm) is disposed within the chamber.

[0009] The thermally conductive substrate is an aluminum nitride ceramic substrate (2mm thick) mounted on the upper surface. A high-power LED array (total power 45W) is mounted on the substrate.

[0010] External cooling fins (12 pieces, height 25mm) are integrally formed on the lower surface of the housing.

[0011] Testing shows that this device maintains the LED junction temperature at 82°C after 2 hours of continuous operation at 45W, which is 10°C lower than a conventional aluminum heat sink of similar size.

【Claims】
1. A thermal management device for a semiconductor light source, comprising:
   a sealed housing defining an internal chamber;
   a phase change material disposed within the internal chamber, the phase change material having a melting point between 40°C and 70°C;
   a thermally conductive substrate mounted on an upper surface of the housing;
   a semiconductor light source mounted on the substrate;
   a plurality of cooling fins integrally formed on an outer surface of the housing.

2. The device of claim 1, wherein the phase change material has a latent heat of fusion of at least 180 kJ/kg.

3. The device of claim 1, further comprising a porous metal foam structure disposed within the internal chamber.

4. The device of claim 1, wherein the thermally conductive substrate is an aluminum nitride ceramic substrate with a thickness of 0.5-3mm.

【Abstract】
A thermal management device for semiconductor light sources includes a sealed housing containing a phase change material, a thermally conductive substrate, and integrally formed cooling fins. The phase change material absorbs heat through phase transition, providing thermal buffering and reducing temperature fluctuations.
